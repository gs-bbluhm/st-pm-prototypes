Three pillars of value

Everything Staircase does rolls up into three outcomes.

Lead with the outcome; the mechanism follows.

Pillar 01

Give time back to your teams

The economic frame: hours saved

Your team stops being researchers and reassemblers of context. The account picture is already current the moment they need it.

90 min → 5 min

Exec briefings drop from ninety minutes to five.

The Monday sweep

A CSM with 50 accounts opens the week already knowing which moved over the weekend, sorted by sentiment change and new events. The first hour goes to deciding what to do, not figuring out what happened.

Meeting prep, 20 minutes to 3

No more searching six tabs and pinging the AE. What has been discussed, what is open, who has gone quiet, all in one place. Read for three minutes, walk in current.

A new CSM inherits 45 accounts in a day

Territory reshuffles historically cost two to four weeks of ramp, and the customer feels the drop. With Staircase the incoming CSM reads each account's history, relationships, and open risks in an afternoon. The customer never notices the handoff.

The habit that unlocks it: before you ask a teammate, Ask Staircase.

Pillar 02

Protect revenue, accelerate growth

The economic frame: revenue protected and expansion captured

A health score that turns red the month before renewal is already too late. A risk signal that arrives with the exact sentence a stakeholder wrote, plus the root cause behind it, is early enough to save the account.

Weeks earlier

Risk surfaced weeks earlier, with the quote that fired it and the root cause behind it.

Caught early enough to save

A champion writes, buried in a long thread, we are being asked to justify this spend in the next budget review. Nobody flags it. Staircase catches the sentence when it is written, ties it to a risk signal with the quote attached, and names the root cause: budget scrutiny, not product dissatisfaction. The CSM opens an executive-alignment play while there is still runway.

Expansion sourced from a signal, not a hunch

A stakeholder mentions a new team or use case on a 40-minute call. The Expansion Analyst tracks it and keeps it warm week over week, so the CSM acts on a specific opening in the customer's own words, and the sourced opportunity is attributable.

Leadership sees the whole book

Risk and expansion roll up across the portfolio, so the forecast conversation is about where to spend the team's attention, with the evidence one click away when a call gets challenged.

Voice of Customer reaches product

The same friction shows up across fifteen accounts. Individually each CSM notes it and moves on. Staircase surfaces the recurring theme with volume and examples, so CS brings product a pattern with evidence, then closes the loop with customers later.

Pillar 03

Make every system customer-aware

The economic frame: intelligence reach and compounding AI value

The intelligence should not live only in Staircase. It should reach the CRM, the data lake, Slack and Teams, and any AI agent your company runs.

Compounding

Every system you make customer-aware saves more time and protects more revenue than Staircase could on its own.

Closed-Loop Orchestration

A risk signal fires, creates a task in your CRM with an owner and a due date, and kicks off the right play. Intelligence becomes action, without anyone having to notice the signal and start the play by hand.

Unified Health View

Most health scores are built on usage and tickets, which miss the relationship. Staircase sentiment and engagement blend with operational health into one score on one view. A green account that has gone quiet and cold in email stops looking green.

Signals where work happens

Events pipe into Slack or Teams, so the team reacts in hours, not at the next weekly review.

Context in any agent, via MCP

The renewal-prep agent your ops team builds, or a CSM's own AI assistant, pulls live account context from Staircase. The more you automate, the more each automation knows.

The value journey

Unlock, Adopt, Prove.

Value is immediate on connection, then deepens on a simple path. This is also a map: any team can locate where it stands today and see the next step.

Step 01

Unlock

Admins, RevOps

Connect comms. Set tiers, topics, and stakeholder roles for your business. Wire notifications into Slack, Teams, and webhooks. Export signals and aggregate data to your data lake so the whole org can use them. Set up the MCP connector and build skills that automate awareness and action.

Step 02

Adopt

CS leaders, managers, CSMs

The team builds the habit: check context before every call, act on the signals. Teammates begin using the intelligence inside their own AI and agent flows, in automated, valuable ways.

Step 03

Prove

Leaders, admins

Measure it: risk caught earlier, hours saved, expansion sourced.
